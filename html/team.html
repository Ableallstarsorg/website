Ball Courts for
Special Needs Kids,
Catch the Family Fun!

Able AllStars, a non-profit 501(c)(3), offers innovative ball courts
tailored for special needs families, merging standard pickleball play with
expanded activity spaces and unique features like smooth walls, music, and spectator seating.
 
 

Coming onboard means joining the family

Able AllStars is a passionate community dedicated to creating inclusive play spaces, led by a diverse and innovative team. Our members range from visionary planners to compassionate volunteers, each contributing unique skills to our mission. Currently, we're building a special pickleball court - a multifunctional space for sports and community engagement. We're committed to a world where play has no barriers, and every child, regardless of ability, can find joy and connection. Your support through donations helps us make this inclusive playground a reality. Join us in our journey to bring joy and inclusivity to every game.

Ted & Karen Drake
FOUNDERS

Karen had an upper management career of almost 40 years in the electrical supply industry, first with General Electric and then with Rexel, USA. This career path moved Ted and Karen across the country more than eight times and allowed for them to discover the east coast, midwest, and west coast lifestyle. Ted's career included sales, publishing, and real estate investments. Both are avid sports enthusiasts and are members of The Stone Canyon Club. Karen was a collegiate golfer while Ted was a competitive water skier as well as a participant in pickleball singles play at the Nationals in 2015 & 2016. Their son, Kyle, and daughter Madison, reside with them in the Northwest Tucson area. The Tucson Autism Society has played a role in the development of their special needs daughter Madison, along with programs such as Creative Care and Arts for All. The hope is to combine our interest and experience in ball sports to further enhance the lives of kids with developmental disabilities by providing ball court venues for local providers of special needs care.

Peter Sullivan
Treasurer

An avid pickler and golfer and has been in finance for over thirty years for Deloitte and in Chief Financial Officer roles for private companies. Peter is a graduate of the University of Washington and the University of Arizona (MBA) and is a CPA. Peter has three children and lives with his wife in Oro Valley, Arizona. View Resume

Jeff Vogan
Board Of Directors

Jeff Vogan, a Registered Investment Advisor and founder of Premiere Retirement Planning and Wealth Management, is deeply committed to serving others both in his professional and personal life. Apart from his financial expertise, which he shares on TV and his radio show, Jeff is also actively involved in the community. He supports various causes such as the Tucson Heart Association, the Autism Society of Southern Arizona, and the Special Olympics. His dedication to charitable work is inspired by his youngest daughter, Megan, who had special needs and passed away early in 2023. Megans life and challenges highlighted the importance of resources for special needs children and their families. Jeff is active in his church and enjoys volunteering in the community. He and his wife, Amy, have been married for over 35 years and are blessed with five children and twelve grandchildren. His hobbies include golf, archery, mountain biking, and other sports. Jeff and his family are enthusiastic supporters of the special-needs community in Tucson and are involved with the Able AllStars organization.
