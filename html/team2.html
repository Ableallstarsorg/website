Ball Courts for
Special Needs Kids,
Catch the Family Fun!

Able AllStars, a non-profit 501(c)(3), offers innovative ball courts
tailored for special needs families, merging standard pickleball play with
expanded activity spaces and unique features like smooth walls, music, and spectator seating.
 
 

Coming onboard means joining the family

Able AllStars is not just an organization but a vibrant community of dedicated team members, each driven by a shared mission: to construct inclusive play spaces that redefine the boundaries of traditional sports. At the heart of our team lies a deep commitment to empowering families with special needs children. We are a diverse group of visionaries, innovators, and compassionate individuals, all united in our goal to create environments where every child, regardless of their abilities, can play, grow, and connect. Our current project, a groundbreaking pickleball court, is a testament to our team's creativity and dedication. This multifunctional space is designed not just for sports but as a hub for community interaction, laughter, and shared experiences. The unique multi-purpose wall that encircles the court symbolizes our team's innovative approachturning a simple sports facility into a canvas for creativity and inclusive play. Every member of the Able AllStars team brings a unique set of skills and perspectives, contributing to our mission of inclusivity. From planners and builders to supporters and volunteers, each person plays a critical role in bringing our vision to lifea vision of a world where play knows no barriers and every game is a celebration of possibility. However, to make this dream a reality, we rely on support from people like you. Your generous, tax-deductible donations are crucial in building this inclusive playground, complete with adjustable nets, audio, and lighting systems. Join us on this journey to create a space where joy is boundless, and every child finds a place to shine. Together, we can make a difference, one game at a time.

Karen Drake & Ted Drake
FOUNDERS

Karen had an upper management career of almost 40 years in the electrical supply industry, first with General Electric and then with Rexel, USA. This career path moved Ted and Karen across the country more than eight times and allowed for them to discover the east coast, midwest, and west coast lifestyle. Ted's career included sales, publishing, and real estate investments. Both are avid sports enthusiasts and are members of The Stone Canyon Club. Karen was a collegiate golfer while Ted was a competitive water skier as well as a participant in pickleball singles play at the Nationals in 2015 & 2016. Their son, Kyle, and daughter Madison, reside with them in the Northwest Tucson area. The Tucson Autism Society has played a role in the development of their special needs daughter Madison, along with programs such as Creative Care and Arts for All. The hope is to combine our interest and experience in ball sports to further enhance the lives of kids with developmental disabilities by providing ball court venues for local providers of special needs care.
